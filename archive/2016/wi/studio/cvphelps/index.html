A Redesign of Reddit's Organization

Navigating Reddit is very straightforward but uninspired. I end up frequenting the same sub-reddits, seeing the same jokes and same reposts over and over again. Reddit really only has two levels of information. First is the sub-reddit, which is a topic or collection. The second is the links, which are posted under each sub-reddit and relate to the overarching theme. For example, a sub-reddit /r/pics would be a list of links to images. This hierarchy is very flat, and has thousands and thousands of links within it. Often there are re-posts across different sub-reddits of the same content, perhaps a link to a cat photo that makes sense on /r/cats, /r/funny, and /r/aww. This process of posting and browsing content could be streamlined and improved in a number of hierarchy and tag-based ways.
